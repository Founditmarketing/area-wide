Built for Lamar County Soil

Why Paris paving needs the right base.

Lamar County sits on a mix of Blackland clay and the Red River bottoms — heavy expansive soil that swells, shrinks, and shifts with every rain and drought cycle. That movement is the #1 reason cheap driveways fail here within five years.

Every Paris project follows the same disciplined four-stage foundation:

Sub-base compaction — Excavation to stable material, then compaction in lifts with vibratory rollers. Skip the base and the surface is dead in three winters.

Engineered grading — Minimum 2% slope on every surface to eliminate standing water, the fastest way to destroy asphalt in Northeast Texas.

Hot-mix asphalt (HMA) — Commercial-grade hot-mix produced at 300°F+ for maximum density and bond strength. Highway-grade material — the same TxDOT specifies for state roads. No cold-patch shortcuts.

Heavy-load construction for ag and ranch work — Lamar County is cattle country. Our pads are built to handle equipment trailers, hay trucks, and skid steers. We don't pour residential-grade asphalt on commercial use cases.

Yes — long rural driveways are a regular part of our Lamar County work. Typical ranch driveways run 200–800 feet. We engineer the base for cattle trailers, hay trucks, and equipment moves, and we grade for proper drainage so the surface doesn't pothole at low spots after rain.

Can you do a full church or school parking lot in Paris?

Yes — churches, schools, and municipal parking lots are some of our most common Paris projects. We handle full tear-out and rebuild, overlays, sealcoating, and ADA-compliant striping. We can phase work to keep parking partially open during construction.

How long does asphalt last in Paris?

Properly installed asphalt lasts 15–20 years in Northeast Texas with sealcoating every 3–5 years. Lamar County's clay soil and freeze-thaw cycles make sub-base preparation the #1 factor in pavement lifespan — which is exactly where we don't cut corners.

When is the best time of year to pave in Paris?

Asphalt installs best between April and October when ambient temps stay above 50°F. Sealcoating requires 60°F+ for 24 hours after application. We pave year-round when conditions allow, but spring and fall produce the most consistent results in Lamar County.
