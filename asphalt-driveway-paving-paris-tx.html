Built for Lamar County

Rural drives, ranch entrances, working farms.

Lamar County has more long rural driveways than most of our service area. We pave 200-foot ranch entrances, half-mile driveways serving rural homes, and traditional in-town residential lots. Heavy-use drives serving farm equipment and trailers get 4 inches of asphalt over 6–8 inches of base — the standard residential 2.5-inch surface won't hold up to repeated heavy traffic.

For city lots in Paris, the standard 2.5–3 inch surface over 4–6 inches of compacted base lasts 15–20 years with sealcoating every 3–5 years. We spec each driveway based on actual use, not a one-size-fits-all formula.

Common Questions

Paris driveway paving, answered.

How much does an asphalt driveway cost in Paris?

$3–$7 per square foot installed. A standard 600 sq ft driveway runs $1,800–$4,200.

Do you serve rural Lamar County properties?

Yes — Reno, Powderly, Blossom, and surrounding rural communities. Long rural drives are common in our quote book.

How thick should a heavy-use driveway be?

For driveways serving farm equipment, RVs, trailers, or heavy ranch use: 4 inches of asphalt over 6–8 inches of compacted base. Standard residential is 2.5–3 inches over 4–6 inches.

How long does paving take?

1–2 days for typical residential. Long rural drives can take 2–3 days. Walkable in 4–6 hours, drivable in 24–48 hours.

Should I overlay or replace?

Overlay if base is solid (saves ~40%). Replace if base has failed. Paul evaluates in person at no charge.
