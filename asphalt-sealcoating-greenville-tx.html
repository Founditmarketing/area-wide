Why It Matters Here

Hunt County clay punishes unsealed asphalt.

Greenville sits on the Blackland Prairie's eastern edge — heavy expansive clay soil that swells and shrinks dramatically with each season. That ground movement, combined with hot summers and freeze-thaw winters, breaks down asphalt binders fast. An unsealed Greenville driveway can lose 5–7 years of service life over its first decade.

Sealcoat replaces those binder oils and locks moisture out of the surface. $0.15–$0.30 per square foot every 3–5 years is the cheapest insurance you can buy on a driveway that costs $5–$9 per square foot to replace.

We use commercial-grade coal tar or asphalt-emulsion sealer applied in two coats. Roughly twice the durability of consumer-grade product for the same labor.

Common Questions

Greenville sealcoating, answered.

How often should I sealcoat in Greenville?

Every 3–5 years. Hunt County's clay soil and seasonal moisture swings make consistent sealcoating critical. New asphalt waits 6–12 months for the first coat.

When is the best time to sealcoat in Greenville?

April through October when ambient temperatures stay above 60°F for 24 hours after application. Spring and fall produce the most consistent results.

Can sealcoating fix my potholes or wide cracks?

No. Sealcoat is preventive maintenance — it protects an intact surface. Cracks larger than 1/8" need hot crack-fill first. Potholes indicate base failure and require patching or replacement before any sealcoat.

How long does sealcoat take to dry?

Walkable in 4–6 hours. Drivable in 24–48 hours. Heavy vehicles or hot tires from a long drive should wait 48 hours minimum.

Can you sealcoat my Greenville commercial parking lot at night?

Yes — for commercial properties we schedule weekend and after-hours work to keep your lot operational. Most retail and medical lots are sealed Friday evening through Sunday morning.
