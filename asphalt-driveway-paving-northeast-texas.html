Engineered for East Texas

Clay soil, summer heat, winter freezes.

Northeast Texas pavement faces three failure modes most other regions don't see in combination: expansive clay soil that moves seasonally (Hunt, Hopkins, Lamar counties), 100°F+ summers that break down asphalt binder, and multiple freeze-thaw cycles each winter that crack improperly compacted bases. Add 35–55 inches of annual rainfall depending on county, and a thin or rushed driveway here lasts 8 years instead of 20.

The fix is non-negotiable: 4–6 inches of compacted aggregate base, properly graded for drainage, under 2.5–3 inches of TxDOT-spec hot-mix asphalt. Heavy-use driveways serving farm equipment, RVs, or repeated trailer use get 4 inches of asphalt over 6–8 inches of base. We spec each driveway based on actual use, not a one-size-fits-all formula.

Common Questions

How much does an asphalt driveway cost?

$3–$7 per square foot installed. Standard 600 sq ft driveway runs $1,800–$4,200. Itemized quotes — no padding.

How long does a driveway last in East Texas?

15–20 years with sealcoating every 3–5 years. Hot-mix engineered for clay soil, summer heat, and freeze-thaw cycles.

How thick should it be?

Standard residential: 2.5–3 inches over 4–6 inches of base. Heavy-use: 4 inches of asphalt over 6–8 inches of base.

Should I overlay or replace?

Overlay if base is solid (saves ~40%). Replace if base has failed (potholes, heaving, alligator cracking). Our estimator evaluates in person at no charge.
