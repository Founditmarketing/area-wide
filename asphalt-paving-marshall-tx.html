Built for Harrison County Soil

Why Marshall paving requires real know-how.

Marshall sits in deep East Texas pine country — sandy loam mixed with red clay pockets, all of it sitting under one of the wetter rainfall belts in the state. Drainage isn't optional here; it's the whole job. Get the grading wrong and the surface ponds, then potholes, then unravels.

Every Marshall project follows the same disciplined four-stage foundation:

Sub-base compaction — Excavation to stable material, then compaction in lifts with vibratory rollers. Especially critical with East Texas's softer subsoils.

Engineered grading — Minimum 2% slope on every surface to eliminate standing water, the fastest way to destroy asphalt in any humid climate.

Hot-mix asphalt (HMA) — Commercial-grade hot-mix produced at 300°F+ for maximum density and bond strength. Highway-grade material — the same TxDOT specs for I-20 and Loop 390. No cold-patch shortcuts.

Edge sealing for humidity — Marshall's wet climate makes edge integrity critical. We seal every joint and transition tight so water doesn't get under the surface and fail the base.

Twenty years of paving across Northeast and East Texas means we've solved the problems Marshall's climate creates. We don't learn on your project.
