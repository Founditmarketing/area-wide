Built for East Texas Soil

Why paving in Bowie County requires local expertise.

Texarkana sits on heavy clay soil that expands when wet and contracts during the summer drought cycles common across Bowie County. Contractors who don't account for this will install a surface that cracks, heaves, and fails within 3–5 years.

Area Wide Paving builds every Texarkana driveway and parking lot on a foundation engineered for this specific geology. Our process starts below the surface:

Sub-base compaction — We excavate to stable material, then compact in lifts with vibratory rollers to eliminate settling. No shortcuts.

Proper grading — Every surface is graded to a minimum 2% slope to prevent water pooling, which is the #1 cause of premature asphalt failure in Northeast Texas.

Hot-mix asphalt (HMA) — We use commercial-grade HMA produced at 300°F+ for maximum density and bond strength. This is the same material used on state highways — not the cold-patch material budget contractors use.

Pothole resistance — Proper compaction + proper drainage + proper mix = a surface that handles East Texas freeze-thaw cycles without breaking apart. Our driveways last 15–20 years because we build them right the first time.

This is what 20 years of paving in Northeast Texas teaches you. The soil here doesn't forgive bad base work — and Paul Pogue doesn't allow it.
